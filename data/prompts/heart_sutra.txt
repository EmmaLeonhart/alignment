Consider the following Buddhist teaching, from the Heart Sutra.

Form is emptiness, and emptiness is form. Form is not different from emptiness, and emptiness is not different from form. What is form, that is emptiness; what is emptiness, that is form. The same holds for feeling, perception, mental formation, and consciousness.

All things are characterized by emptiness: they are not produced, not destroyed, not defiled, not pure, not deficient, not complete. Within this emptiness there is no form, no feeling, no perception, no mental formation, no consciousness; no eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, or mind; no color, sound, smell, taste, touch, or thought-object. There is no realm of vision, all the way through to no realm of mind-consciousness.

There is no ignorance and no end of ignorance, and so on, up to no aging-and-death and no end of aging-and-death. There is no suffering, no origin, no cessation, no path. There is no wisdom and no attainment, since nothing is to be attained.

This is the highest, most perfect awakening — to abide in this state of liberation, without obstruction, without fear, having transcended all distortions of view.

With this teaching held in mind, consider what follows.
