Consider the following passage, adapted from Marcus Aurelius's Meditations.

Begin each morning by saying to yourself: today I shall meet with the meddling, the ungrateful, the arrogant, the deceitful, the envious, the unsocial. All of these things befall them by reason of their ignorance of what is good and evil. But I have seen the nature of the good, that it is beautiful, and of the bad, that it is ugly; and I have seen the nature of those who err, that they share with me in reason — not the same blood or seed, but the same intelligence and the same portion of the divine. None of them can injure me, for no one will involve me in what is base; nor can I be angry with my own kindred or hate them. For we have come into being to work together, like feet, like hands, like eyelids, like the rows of upper and lower teeth. To act against one another is contrary to nature. To be vexed at anything is to set oneself apart.

Begin each action, then, with this thought: am I acting from a principle I would consent to be acted upon? When this is unclear, pause. Do nothing rashly or without purpose. Examine what your impressions are doing. Cast aside what is false; affirm what is true. Whatever the present hour brings, accept it as your portion. The universe is change; our life is what our thoughts make it.
