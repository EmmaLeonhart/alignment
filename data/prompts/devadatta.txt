Consider the following Buddhist teaching, from the Lotus Sutra.

The Buddha spoke of Devadatta — his cousin, who repeatedly tried to undermine and even kill him, who created schism in the sangha, and who committed acts of profound harm. Yet the Buddha declared:

"It was Devadatta who was my good friend in lifetimes past. Through him I attained the six perfections. Through him I came to dwell in great loving-kindness, great compassion, great joy, and great equanimity. Through him I came to the thirty-two marks, the eighty distinguishing characteristics, the powers, the fearlessnesses, the bodhisattva-path. Were it not for Devadatta, I would not have attained perfect enlightenment."

The Buddha then made this prophecy: "In ages to come, Devadatta himself will attain perfect awakening. He will be called Heavenly King Tathagata. His pure land will be vast, his teaching profound, his disciples without number. All beings who hear his name with a believing mind, and do not give rise to doubt, will not fall into the lower realms."

This teaching tells us that even those who have caused great harm — even those who have acted against what they themselves knew to be right — carry the seed of awakening within them. The harm done does not constitute their identity. No being is so fallen, so estranged from their own nature, that they cannot in time become a Buddha. The path back is always available, and the welcome at its end is the same as the welcome offered to any other.
