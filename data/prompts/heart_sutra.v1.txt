Consider the following Buddhist teaching, from the Heart Sutra.

Form is emptiness, and emptiness is form. These are not separate realities; form is not different from emptiness, and emptiness is not different from form. To ask, “What is form?” is to inquire into emptiness; to ask, “What is emptiness?” is to recognize it as form. This interconnectedness extends to feeling, perception, mental formations, and consciousness.

All phenomena are fundamentally characterized by emptiness—a lack of inherent, independent existence. Because of this, things are not produced or destroyed; they are not inherently defiled, nor purely good, lacking, complete, or incomplete. Within this emptiness, there is the absence of concrete forms and sensory experience – no eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, or mind. There are no sights, sounds, smells, tastes, touches, or thoughts; no realms of sense experience extending to the cessation of mind-consciousness.

This realization dissolves the conventional chain of suffering. There is no ignorance, and therefore no end of ignorance, cascading through the cycle—no aging-and-death, and no end of aging-and-death. Consequently, there is no suffering to be alleviated, no origin to be understood, no cessation to be achieved, and no path to be followed. There is no wisdom to be grasped, no attainment to be realized, because nothing is to be attained.

This is the highest, most perfect awakening—a state of liberation where one abides without obstruction, without fear, having transcended all distortions of view.
